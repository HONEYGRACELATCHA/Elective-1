CPU (Central Processing Unit)

A Central Processing Unit (CPU) is the primary component of a computer system responsible for controlling the interpretation and execution of instructions. It is the “control center” of the computer, executing software instructions, such as arithmetic, logic, controlling, and input/output (I/O) operations.

Computer Case

A computer case, also known as a computer chassis, tower, system unit, or cabinet, is the enclosure that contains most of the components of a personal computer (usually excluding the display, keyboard, and mouse). Its primary function is to protect the computer’s components from harm, such as electrical interference, physical damage, and intrusive foreign objects.

Monitor

A PC monitor, also known as a computer display or video display, is an electronic device that displays visual information produced by a computer’s video card. It is a type of output device that connects to a computer via various ports such as VGA, HDMI, DVI, DisplayPort, or USB-C.

Keyboard

A keyboard is a device consisting of a set of keys or buttons arranged in a specific layout, used for inputting characters, symbols, and commands into a computer, typewriter, or other electronic device. The keyboard serves as a primary input method, allowing users to interact with the device and communicate through text.

A computer mouse is a handheld hardware input device used to control a cursor on a graphical user interface (GUI). It detects two-dimensional motion relative to a surface, typically a flat surface such as a mouse pad or desk, and translates this motion into the movement of the cursor on the display.
